  <h1>7 Wonders of the World</h1>
  <ul>
    <li>The Great Wall of China</li><br>
<img src="wall.jpg" width="125" height="125">
The wall was built from as early as the 7th century BC,
 with selective stretches later joined by Qin Shi Huang (220–206 BC),
 the first emperor of China. Little of the Qin wall remains. Later on,
 many successive dynasties built and maintained multiple stretches of border walls.
 The best-known sections of the wall were built by the Ming dynasty (1368–1644) ¹³.
 The Great Wall of China is approximately 21,196.18 km (13,170.70 mi) long in total.
<br><br>

    <li>Petra</li><br>
<img src="petra.jpg" width="125" height="125">
Petra is an ancient city located in the Ma'an Governorate of Jordan.
 It is believed to have been built as early as the 5th century BC. 
The city was the center of an Arab kingdom in Hellenistic and Roman times. 
Petra is famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system.
 <br><br>

    <li>Christ the Redeemer</li><br>
<img src="redeemer.jpg" width="125" height="125">
Christ the Redeemer is a colossal statue of Jesus Christ 
located at the summit of Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
 The statue stands 98 feet (30 meters) tall and has horizontally
 outstretched arms spanning **92 feet (28meters)**.
 <br><br>

    <li>Machu Picchu</li><br>
<img src="machu pichu.jpg" width="125" height="125"> 
Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel located in the Eastern Cordillera
 of southern Peru on a 2,430-meter (7,970 ft) mountain ridge .
 It is often referred to as the "Lost City of the Incas" and is the most familiar icon of the Inca Empire .
<br><br>

    <li>Chichen Itza</li><br>
<img src="chichen.jpg" width="125" height="125">
Chichén Itzá is a large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya people 
of the Terminal Classic period. The archeological site is located in Tinúm Municipality, 
Yucatán State, Mexico ². The city was once a center of culture and religion for the Maya 
people and is thought to have been a religious, military, political, and commercial center 
that at its peak would have been home to 35,000 people ³. 
<br><br>

    <li>The Colosseum</li><br>
<img src="colosseum.jpg" width="125" height="125"> 
The Colosseum is an ancient amphitheater located in Rome, Italy.
 It was built during the Flavian dynasty in the first century AD
 and is considered one of the greatest works of Roman architecture.
<br><br>

    <li>Taj Mahal</li><br>
<img src="taj mahal.jpg" width="125" height="125"> 
The Taj Mahal is a mausoleum complex located in Agra, 
Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned by the fifth Mughal emperor,
 Shah Jahan, in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth
 in 1631¹²³. The construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653¹.
<br><br>
